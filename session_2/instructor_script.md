---

## 00:10 - 00:25 | The Ghost in the Machine: Hallucinations and Limits

**Speaker Notes:**  
"The most dangerous thing about modern AI is that it ever says 'I don't know' unless it’s specifically programmed to. Instead, it **hallucinates**.

Why does this happen? Remember what we said last week: AI is a probability engine. If you ask it for the biography of a person who doesn't exist, it will look at the *most likely* words that follow 'Born in...' and 'Educated at...'. It’s not checking a database of facts; it’s painting a picture with words.

**Hallucinations:**  
'I once asked an AI for a list of 10 books on Italian cooking written in the 1800s. It gave me 10 titles. 4 of them were real. 6 were completely made up, including the names of the authors. They sounded perfect. They were formatted perfectly. But they did not exist.'

**Logic and Math:**  
'AI also struggles with what we call 'spatial reasoning' and complex math. If you ask it 'If I have 3 oranges and I put them in a box, then I take out 2 and put in 5, how many are left?', it might get it right. But if the logic gets layered, it starts to trip. It sees the words, but it doesn't 'experience' the box.'

**Bias:**  
'And finally, there's Bias. The AI was trained on the internet. And as we know, the internet isn't always fair, objective, or kind. If the data it learned from has prejudices, the AI will reflect them. We have to be the 'Moral Compass' for the machine.'"

---

## 00:25 - 00:45 | The Pilot’s Strategy: Multi-Model and Context

**Speaker Notes:**  
"So, how do we use this thing if we can't trust it 100%? We become **Strategists**.

1. **Multi-Model Usage:**  
'Don't get married to one model. If I’m writing code or doing deep logic, I might use **Claude**. If I want a quick summary or a creative brainstorm, I might use **GPT-4o**. If I need something integrated with my spreadsheets, I use **Gemini**. Think of them as different consultants. If a doctor gives you a weird diagnosis, you get a second opinion. Do the same with AI.'

2. **The Power of Context:**  
'The biggest mistake people make is being too brief. 'Write me an email' is a bad prompt. 'Write me a professional yet warm email to a client in Tilburg regarding a 2-day delay in their furniture delivery. Mention that we are offering a 10% discount for the trouble. Keep it under 100 words.'—that is a pilot's prompt. You are giving the machine the boundaries it needs to stay on track.'

3. **Web Research vs. Knowledge Base:**  
'Tools like **Perplexity** or **SearchGPT** actually browse the live web. This reduces hallucinations because the machine has to 'cite its sources.' Always check the links!'"

---

## 00:45 - 00:55 | The Prompt Challenge: Vague vs. Detailed

**Activity:**
"Let's look at the screen. I'm going to type 'Write a travel plan for Tilburg.' Look at the result. It's okay, but generic. Now, watch as I add: 'You are a local expert. Write a 4-hour walking tour for a family with a toddler, focusing on parks and affordable cafes.'
Notice the difference? The machine didn't get smarter; the instruction got better."

---

## 00:55 - 01:20 | Practical Activity: Pair Work and Individual Tasks

**Activity:**
"Now, find a partner. We’re doing the **Pair Challenge**. One of you writes a 'lazy' prompt. The other has to 'save' it by adding context, tasks, and format. Once you're done, you can move on to the Individual Tasks in your exercise sheet: The Translator, The Debugger, or The Analyst.

I’ll be walking around to help you 'debug' your prompts."

---

## 01:20 - 01:30 | Interfaces: Online vs. IDE (Antigravity)

**Speaker Notes:**  
"Before we wrap up, I want to show you one more thing. Most of you use the 'Chat' window. But for professionals, there is a new way: **Agentic IDEs**.

Take **Antigravity**, for example. It’s not just a chat. It’s an AI that has 'eyes' on my files. It can see the folders I’m creating for this course, it can run commands, and it can suggest changes directly inside the documents. It’s the difference between talking to a consultant on the phone and having an assistant sitting right next to you at your desk.

As we move toward the 'Grand Laboratory' in the next two sessions, think about which interface suits your project better. Do you want to talk to the AI, or do you want to work *with* it?"
