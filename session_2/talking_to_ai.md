# Session 2: Talking to AI

This session is dedicated to practice and understanding the "character" of AI.

## Roadmap (90 Minutes)

- **00-10 min:** Recap of the previous week and feedback.
- **10-25 min:** Theory: Hallucinations, Errors, Limits, and Bias.
- **25-45 min:** Strategies: Multi-model, Web Research, Context.
- **45-55 min:** **Prompt Challenge:** Vague vs. Detailed prompts.
- **55-80 min:** **Practical Activity:** Pair work and rewriting.
- **80-90 min:** Results sharing and group discussion.

## 1. Deal with it: Addressing Limits

- **Hallucinations:** Why does AI invent facts with conviction?
- **Logic Errors:** Limits in mathematical or spatial reasoning.
- **Bias:** Prejudices present in training data.

## 2. Advanced Strategies

- **Multi-modality:** Using Claude for code and GPT for logic (or vice versa).
- **Web Research:** How integrated searches work (and where they fail) (Perplexity, SearchGPT).
- **Context:** The importance of providing background examples and data.

## 3. Comparing Interfaces

- **Online (Chat):** Simple, fast, great for general tasks.
- **IDE / Agentic (Antigravity/Cursor):** Watching the AI work directly on files, executing commands, and fixing bugs.

## 4. Practical Activities

- **Hands-on Round 1:** Ask a free AI chatbot a simple question (rapid question-answering).
- **Prompt Challenge:** Compare a vague prompt vs. a detailed one (Context + Task + Format).
- **Pair Work:** Rewrite a "bad" prompt together with a partner and share the results.
- **Individual Tasks:** Choose one from the exercise list (Translator, Debugger, Analyst).
