# Instructor Script: Sessions 3 & 4 - The Grand Laboratory

**Location:** International Center of Tilburg  
**Duration:** 180 Minutes (90 + 90)  
**Language:** English  

---

## SESSION 3: PLANNING AND LAUNCH (90 MINUTES)

### 00:00 - 00:10 | Recap: Mistakes to Avoid

"Before we jump into the lab, let's look at the three most common mistakes from last week: being too vague, trusting facts blindly, and forgetting to provide context. Keep these in mind today."

---

### 00:10 - 00:30 | The Showcase: Beyond the Chatbox

"Today I want to show you that AI isn't just a text box.

- We have **ElevenLabs**, which can clone a voice or translate a video while keeping your voice.
- We have **OpenArt**, which is like having a digital artist at your command.
- And for the tech-savvy, we have **Windsurf** or **Antigravity**, tools that don't just talk to you but actually *work* on your files with you."

---

### 00:30 - 00:45 | The Truth Gap: Fact-Checking

"One warning before you start: AI is a liar by nature. It's built for *plausibility*, not *accuracy*. If you're building a project that depends on facts—dates, names, historical events—you **must** verify them. Use Perplexity or Google to confirm what the AI tells you. Don't be the pilot who flies into a mountain because the GPS said it wasn't there."

---

### 00:45 - 01:00 | The Vision: Choosing your Everest

"In this room, we have a wealth of different backgrounds, interests, and problems to solve. Some of you want to automate your office work. Some want to write a novel. Some want to build a website for their hobby. Today is the day you stop asking 'Can it do this?' and start saying 'AI, help me build this.'

Your goal for the next 15 minutes is simple: **Choose your Everest.** It doesn't have to be a big Everest—it can be a small hill. But it must be yours. Think about a task that usually takes you 5 hours. Can we do it in 2 with AI? Think about a skill you don’t have—like coding or graphic design. Can the AI bridge that gap?"

---

### 01:00 - 01:15 | The Architecture: Building the Map

"Before you start typing, you need a plan. If you go to an AI and say 'Build me a website,' it will give you something generic and likely broken.

Use these 15 minutes to **create an Outline** with the AI.

1. Describe your goal to the AI.
2. Ask it: 'What are the steps I need to take to achieve this?'
3. Ask it: 'What information do I need to provide you to make this successful?'

By the end of this 15-minute block, I want you to have a numbered list of tasks. This is your 'Flight Plan.'"

---

### 01:15 - 02:15 (Approx) | The Deep Work: Implementation

"Now, we go into 'Deep Work' mode. I will be circulating around the room.

**Advice for this hour:**

- **Start Small:** If you’re building a website, start with the 'About Me' page.
- **Fail Fast:** If the AI gives you code that doesn’t work, ask it why immediately.
- **Interface Choice:** Use the best tool for the job.

GO."

---

## SESSION 4: FINALIZATION AND DEMO (90 MINUTES)

### 00:00 - 01:00 | The Final Sprint: Polishing the Diamond

**Speaker Notes:**  
"Welcome up to our final session. You have one hour left of 'Lab Time.'

**Today’s priority is Finalization.**
It is better to have one perfectly working feature than ten broken ones. Use the AI today as an **Editor** and a **Debugger**. Ask it: 'Review what we’ve built. What are we missing? How can we make the language more professional?'

At the 60-minute mark, we stop. No exceptions."

---

### 01:00 - 01:15 | The Mirror: Reflection Phase

**Speaker Notes:**  
"Step away from the screens. Take a breath. Look at what you’ve done.

For the next 15 minutes, I want you to write down three things:

1. One thing the AI did that was **much easier** than you expected.
2. One thing that was **frustratingly difficult**.
3. How your project would be different without these tools.

Understanding the *limitations* you encountered today is what makes you a professional AI user."

---

### 01:15 - 01:30 | The Stage: Flash Demos & Closing

**Speaker Notes:**  
"We are going to do 'Flash Demos.' You have 90 seconds to 2 minutes.

- What was the goal?
- Show us the result.
- What was the 'Aha!' moment?

*(Conduct the presentations. Keep the energy high.)*

**Closing Remarks:**  
'The world is moving fast. The mindset you learned here—how to break down problems, how to provide context, and how to critically evaluate output—that mindset is permanent.

Thank you for being part of this first AI cohort at the International Center of Tilburg. The lab is never truly closed.'"
