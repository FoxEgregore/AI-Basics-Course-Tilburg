## 00:10 - 00:30 | A Brief History: From Turing to Transformers

**The Dawn (1950s):**  
"The story doesn’t start with ChatGPT. It starts in 1950 with **Alan Turing**. He asked a simple, haunting question: *'Can machines think?'* He proposed the Turing Test—if a human talking to a machine via text can’t tell it's a machine, we should consider it 'intelligent.'

In 1956, a group of scientists met at **Dartmouth College**. This was the official birth of 'Artificial Intelligence' as a field. They were incredibly optimistic. They thought that in a single summer, they could solve most of AI. They were wrong.

**The Winters:**  
"For the next few decades, the field went through 'AI Winters.' Funding dried up because the machines weren't smart enough. They were based on logic and rules—if/then statements. But humans don’t work that way. We don’t follow rigid rules; we recognize patterns."

**The Modern Era (2012 - 2017):**  
"The real breakthrough happened in 2012 with **AlexNet**. Suddenly, computers became better than humans at recognizing images of cats, dogs, and cars. Why? Because of **Deep Learning**. We stopped giving the machine rules and started giving it *examples*.

Then came 2017. A team at Google published a paper titled **'Attention is All You Need.'** This introduced the **Transformer**. This architecture is what powers everything you see today. It allowed machines to understand the *context* of a word based on the words around it. It didn't just see 'Apple' as a fruit; it saw 'Apple' as a company if the word 'iPhone' was nearby. This changed everything."

## 00:30 - 00:45 | The AI Glossary: Speaking the Language

**Speaker Notes:**  
"Before we go further, we need to speak the same language. You’ll hear these terms everywhere, so let's break them down simply.

1. **LLM (Large Language Model):** This is the brain. It’s 'Large' because it’s trained on almost the entire internet. It’s a 'Language Model' because its only job is to predict the next word in a sequence. It’s like a super-powered version of the auto-complete on your phone.
2. **Prompt:** This is your instruction. In the old days, we wrote 'code.' Now, we write 'prompts.' The quality of what you get out is directly proportional to the quality of what you put in.
3. **Token:** Think of these as pieces of words. AI doesn’t see 'Hello.' it sees a numerical representation of 'Hel' and 'lo.' Understanding tokens helps you understand why AI sometimes struggles with counting letters or spelling—it’s seeing snippets, not letters.
4. **Generative AI (GenAI):** This is the category. Most AI in the past was *discriminative* (is this a cat or a dog?). Modern AI is *generative* (create me a picture of a cat playing a piano). It’s creating something brand new from its training data."

## 00:55 - 01:15 | The Ecosystem: Who is Who?

**Speaker Notes:**  
"It feels like every week a new AI comes out. Let's map the world of 2024-2025.

**OpenAI (ChatGPT):**  
They started the fire. ChatGPT is the all-rounder. It’s user-friendly, very polished, and usually the benchmark for logic and reasoning.

**Anthropic (Claude):**  
Founded by former OpenAI employees who were worried about safety. Claude is known for being more 'human' in its writing. It’s less robotic than ChatGPT and has a massive 'context window'—you can feed it an entire book and ask it questions.

**Google (Gemini):**
The titan. Gemini’s strength is integration. It’s connected to your Gmail, your Docs, and your Maps.

**Qwen & Deepseek:**
Don't overlook the newer players. **Qwen** is incredible for multilingual tasks, and **Deepseek** is proving that efficient, open-weights models can rival the giants.

## 01:15 - 01:25 | AI in the Shadows: Privacy & Everyday Life

**Speaker Notes:**
"Before we leave, we need to address the two biggest elephants in the room: Privacy and where AI already exists.

**Everyday AI:**
You might think AI is new, but you've been using it for years. When Google Maps tells you to take a different street because of traffic, that's AI. When Netflix suggests a movie you actually end up liking, that's AI. When your phone recognizes your face, that's AI. It's just that now, the AI is *talking back* to us.

**Privacy:**
This is critical. Treat an AI chat like a conversation in a public park. Don't whisper your passwords, your bank details, or your deep company secrets into it. Most free models use your conversations to 'learn.' If you wouldn't want it on a billboard, don't put it in the prompt."
